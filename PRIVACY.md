# Privacy Policy (Unified Game Library)

Last updated: 2026-05-07

This Privacy Policy explains what data the Unified Game Library application (“App”) processes.

## 1) Summary
- **No account login required by default**: The App primarily discovers games from local files/manifests on your PC.
- **No selling of personal data**: The App does not sell personal data.
- **Local-first**: Custom entries and settings are stored locally on your device.

## 2) Data the App may read on your device
To provide functionality, the App may read:
- **Launcher manifests and install metadata** (e.g., Steam library manifests, Epic `.item` manifests)
- **Start Menu shortcuts** (to list “Other” apps/games)
- **Game configuration files** when you choose to optimize a game (e.g., Unreal `GameUserSettings.ini`)
- **VR application settings files** when you choose to apply VR profiles (e.g., SteamVR settings, Virtual Desktop settings, Vive settings), if present
- **Windows settings** relevant to optional PC tweaks (registry values, power plan status, networking settings), when you choose to apply those tweaks

## 3) Data the App stores
The App may store locally:
- **Custom game entries** (name, executable path, arguments, working directory)
- **Your App preferences** (e.g., UI settings, toggles)
- **Backup snapshots** of files/settings before applying opt-in tweaks (for reversibility)

These are stored in your user App Data folders (platform-dependent).

## 4) Data the App sends over the network
By default, the App does **not** need to send your personal data to external servers to function.

If future versions add optional API-based integrations (for example, signing into a launcher account), those features will be clearly labeled and may require communicating with the relevant launcher provider’s services.

## 5) Telemetry and analytics
Unless explicitly enabled and disclosed in the UI, the App does **not** collect telemetry/analytics.

## 6) Security
No method of storage is perfectly secure. The App aims to minimize sensitive data storage and to keep data local where possible.

## 7) Your choices
- You can remove custom games and backups by deleting them from the App’s data directory.
- You can avoid applying any tweaks by not using the optimization features.

## 8) Contact
If you have privacy questions, contact the distributor/maintainer of your copy of the App.
